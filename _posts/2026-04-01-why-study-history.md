date: 2026-04-01

I've been thinking about why history matters, especially as a CS student. You'd think I'd spend all my time thinking about code and algorithms, but honestly, learning about history gives me a lot of perspective.

Understanding how past civilizations solved problems — from Roman engineering to the logistics of the Silk Road — shows that humans have always been innovators. The challenges change, but the creativity stays the same.

Plus, a lot of modern technology has roots in historical events. The internet itself came out of Cold War defense research. GPS was originally a military project. Even the concept of a "computer" goes back to Charles Babbage in the 1800s.

History isn't just about memorizing dates — it's about understanding patterns and learning from both successes and failures.
